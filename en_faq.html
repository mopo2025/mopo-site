LESSON

Lesson-related

LESSONLesson-related
Do you offer half-day lessons?

We currently offer full-day lessons only. The pace is more comprehensive and both safety and quality are more consistent.

What languages do you support?

We support Mandarin, Cantonese, English, and Japanese for smooth communication.

Should I choose skiing or snowboarding?

Pick the one that excites you!

Snowboard
Like surfing or skateboarding—you may fall more at first, but once it clicks you can play with terrain and tricks freely.

Ski
Similar to inline or ice skating—more stable from day one, so you’ll feel the joy of sliding quickly. With progress, you can express elegant lines and precise control.

Can snowboarding and skiing be taught together in one lesson?

No. Posture, balance, and movement mechanics differ greatly, and instructors must use the same equipment as the learner for correct demos and spotting. Mixing both in one session causes conflicting cues and reduces safety and effectiveness. Learn separately first—then practice together on the hill once the basics are in place.

What’s included in the lesson fee?

Instructor fees are included. Lift tickets, rentals, lunch, and transportation to/from the resort are not included.

I’m a first-timer. How many days should I book?

Day 1 is great for learning to stop safely. With 2–3 days, most learners can link stable turns and manage speed. Schedule at a comfortable pace—adding a rest day between lessons is helpful if you’re concerned about fatigue.

What’s the maximum group size per instructor?

Up to 5 learners per instructor. For effectiveness, we recommend grouping people of similar level.

If everyone is a beginner, groups of 4 or fewer reduce waiting and increase practice time.

Children under 7 are taught one-on-one for safety and quality.

Lesson duration and meeting details?

Duration
Full-day lessons are about 6 hours (including a 1-hour lunch). Your instructor will pace the day to match energy and progress. If a child is attending without a guardian, lunch time and location can be arranged in advance.

Meeting point
Usually the resort lodge or a designated meeting spot. Your instructor will reach out on the morning of the lesson and share a photo of their outfit so you can recognize them easily.

Resort lesson hours
Sapporo Teine: 09:30–15:30
Sapporo Kokusai: 09:30–15:30

From what age can kids join?

Our kids’ lessons are designed for ages 4–6 (pre-school) and are always one-on-one for safety and peace of mind.

For kids, start with skis or a snowboard?

We generally recommend starting with skis for younger children—fewer falls and faster wins help build confidence. Experience with inline or ice skating also translates well to skiing.

Kids who love sideways board sports may enjoy snowboarding, but for most under age 7, skiing is usually the safer, smoother start.

Any age limits or safety considerations?

No legal age limit, but safe, enjoyable learning depends on physical condition, coordination, focus, and risk management.

Suggested starting ages: 4+ for skiing; 7+ for snowboarding.

Beginners aged 60–64: If you’re new to sideways sports, we recommend starting on skis. Please consult your physician; private lessons and adequate protective gear improve safety.

Age 65+: Starting as a brand-new beginner may not be suitable for safety reasons. If you already have experience, we can discuss advanced, tailored options.

What if the weather or snow is bad—how are fees handled?

If the resort officially suspends lift operations for the entire day due to unavoidable circumstances, we will process refunds in accordance with our Cancellation & Refund Policy . Please contact us if anything is unclear.

Should I buy a lift ticket in advance?

For first-timers, no need. We’ll recommend the most suitable ticket type based on the day’s plan.

Advanced riders who expect frequent lift use may benefit from purchasing in advance.

Do you offer group lessons?

For this season we focus on private lessons only. If we open group lessons in the future, we’ll announce them on our website and social channels.

GEARGear-related
Can I rent equipment and outerwear?

Yes. We can refer you to on-mountain rental counters and nearby shops, and provide sizing guidance. You can rent skis/snowboard, boots, poles, plus outerwear like jackets and pants.

If instructor checks or adjustments are needed, we recommend the resort’s own rental counter for quick swaps.
※ For Sapporo Kokusai lessons, rentals are limited to the official on-site counter.

Can I rent impact shorts, pads, goggles, or gloves?

Protective gear (impact shorts, knee pads, wrist guards, etc.)
Due to hygiene and fit, we recommend bringing your own. For snowboard beginners, full protection is strongly recommended. Try on outerwear with pads to ensure sizing is comfortable and mobile.

Goggles & gloves
Some shops rent or sell them but stock is limited—please bring your own or reserve in advance. Glasses wearers should look for OTG (Over-The-Glasses) goggles.

Sizing tips for rentals?

Skis/board: based on height and level.
Boots: snug to foot length without pain.
Poles: elbows at ~90° when holding the grips.
When in doubt, your instructor can advise on the day.

How are ski/snowboard boots different from regular shoes?

Ski and snowboard boots connect to bindings to support your body and transmit power.

Ski boots: rigid shells that firmly support the ankle/shin and lock into ski bindings.
Snowboard boots: more flexible for bending and movement control.

Hiking shoes or sneakers cannot attach to bindings and lack required support—use proper boots for safety.

Is a beanie enough—can I skip a helmet and pads?

We strongly recommend helmets and they are required during lessons to protect against falls or collisions.

For snowboarding, we also recommend impact shorts, wrist guards, and knee pads—beginners commonly injure wrists and the lower back/hip area. Protective gear reduces risk and helps you learn with confidence.

RENTALRental-related
Do I need to reserve rentals in advance?

During peak periods and weekends, please reserve in advance. On weekdays you may rent on site, but popular sizes sell out quickly—reserve ahead to be safe.

International visitors are often asked for photo ID at rental counters; bringing your passport speeds up the process.

What’s the rental process?

Provide height/weight/foot size → try boots → select skis/board & poles → staff adjust bindings (angles/DIN) → gear check & signature.

※ For snowboard beginners, a duck stance (symmetrical angles) is often easier—ask the shop staff.

TRANSPORTTransport-related
Do you provide transportation?

We do not provide direct transportation due to safety and insurance compliance. Some schools do, but non-compliant operations may void coverage in case of accidents.

Please use resort shuttles, public buses, or taxis. We’re happy to help check routes and timetables.

How do I get from central Sapporo to Sapporo Teine?

• JR: Sapporo Station → Hakodate Line to Teine Station (~15 min) → bus or taxi to the resort (~16 min).

• Bus: Direct hotel pickups operated by BIGRUNS (reservation required).

• Car: ~40 min from downtown. Parking available (may fill during peak). Winter roads can be icy—snow driving experience recommended.

How do I get from Sapporo to Onze Ski Resort?

No-Cruz Onze sits along National Route 5 toward Otaru, ~40 min by car from central Sapporo.

Nearest IC: Sapporo–Otaru Expressway “Zenibako IC,” with quick access after exit. Free parking.

Public transit: JR from Sapporo to “Zenibako” (~20 min), then taxi (~10 min) or the resort’s free shuttle.

Enjoy sweeping views over Ishikari Bay, and night skiing runs until 23:00—one of its biggest draws.

How do I get from central Sapporo to Mt. Moiwa Ski Resort?

It’s a metropolitan-area resort ~30 minutes by car from downtown, with free parking.

Public transit: Subway Namboku Line from Sapporo/Odori to “Makomanai” (~20 min),

then a direct Jotetsu bus to the ski area (~20 min).

On weekends/holidays during the season, direct shuttles often run—check schedules in advance. Close to downtown yet rich in nature with varied terrain.

How do I get from central Sapporo to Sapporo Kokusai?

• Direct bus: from Sapporo Station (North Exit) or Odori Park (Odori Nishi 1) via the Sapporo Kokusai Ski Line (~90 min / reservation required).

• Car: ~2 hr 15 min from downtown (weather dependent). Recommended only for drivers with ample winter experience.

How do I get from central Sapporo to Rusutsu?

• Bus: Direct services from Sapporo Station / New Chitose Airport (~2.5 hr / reservation required), e.g., BIGRUNS.

• Car: ~2 hr 15 min from downtown (conditions vary with weather). Recommended only for experienced winter drivers.

TRAVELTravel-related
What else should I see and do besides skiing?

Add some city strolls and scenic stops after riding:

• Near Sapporo Teine: Head back downtown for Odori Park, museums, and Shiroi Koibito Park. In February, the Sapporo Snow Festival is a must.

• Near Sapporo Kokusai: Walk the Otaru Canal and Sakaimachi Street for sweets and souvenirs. For night views, try Mt. Tengu.

• Near Rusutsu: Soak with lake views at Toya Onsen; on clear days, Showa-Shinzan and the Usuzan Ropeway offer superb vistas.

When is the ski season?

Typical Hokkaido season: late Nov – early Apr. Higher resorts may run into May.

Nov–early Dec snowfall can be inconsistent; lower resorts may open intermittently. For reliable conditions, plan your visit by early April.

After April, only some higher resorts operate and terrain trends more intermediate/advanced (some short hikes may be needed).

Spring snow is softer and slower—great for relaxed cruising with gentler falls.

Check our website and IG / FB / Threads / X for the latest snow and lesson information.

Onsen near Sapporo (1): Jozankei Onsen

Jozankei lies in Sapporo’s Minami Ward, nestled in snowy valleys within Shikotsu-Toya National Park—perfect winter healing.

The chloride springs help ease muscle fatigue and warm cold hands and feet. Outdoor baths offer magical snowfall views.

Footbaths and historic spots dot the area, and Sapporo Kokusai is ~20 minutes by car for premium powder.

Ride Kokusai by day, then stay in Jozankei and unwind in a snowy open-air bath at night—peak winter vibes.

Onsen near Sapporo (2): Teine Onsen

Teine Onsen sits at the foot of Mt. Teine in Sapporo’s Nishi Ward. It’s a local, day-use style spot rather than a hot-spring town.

The gentle simple springs warm you through and ease post-ride fatigue. Winter views toward Mt. Teine are simple but refreshing.

Best for drivers: ski Sapporo Teine in the morning, soak on the way back, then return to your lodging relaxed.

Onsen near Sapporo (3): Koganeyu Onsen

Koganeyu is a small, quiet onsen ~10 minutes by car from Jozankei, with origins back to the Meiji era and a nature-wrapped atmosphere.

Simple sulfur springs leave skin silky and help with fatigue and relaxation; winter baths in the snowy valley are delightful.

There are only a few inns, but the vibe is warm and calm. Close to Sapporo, great for a day trip or a quiet overnight.
