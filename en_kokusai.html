Sapporo

Sapporo Kokusai

Just an hour from the city, it opens up a snow-filled escape.

Set high in the mountains above Jozankei Onsen, it’s a resort famous for deep, consistent powder and wide-open runs. Whether you’re carving cruisers, chasing fresh lines, or bringing the family for a day on the slopes, Kokusai blends easy access with the feel of a true alpine getaway.

— Resort overview — by train & bus — by car

Resort overview

There are seven trails in total, with a balanced mix of about 30% beginner, 40% intermediate, and 30% advanced. Wide, gentle runs make the resort approachable for newcomers and families, while the 3.6 km top-to-base course offers a satisfying cruise for those who want longer laps.

Known for its heavy snowfall, Kokusai also keeps several powder zones ungroomed, giving advanced skiers and snowboarders the chance to enjoy natural terrain and softer turns.

The slopes see a near even mix of skiers and snowboarders, with both local riders and visitors from overseas adding to the atmosphere. Its proximity to the city makes it an easy option for a full day on the mountain without the long travel.

by train & bus

✦ Direct shuttle buses
Direct buses operate from Sapporo Station and major city hotels. Services require advance reservation, and package options are available that include both a lift ticket and round-trip transport.

Click to enlarge

✦ Jozankei Onsen connection
From Jozankei Onsen, local buses also connect directly to the resort. Many visitors combine a ski day with a relaxing soak in the hot springs—a classic “ski & onsen” experience.

by car

The resort has free parking for about 1,200 cars. While the area sees heavy snowfall, main roads are well maintained. We recommend winter‑driving experience for safety.

  • About 90 minutes from New Chitose Airport (via expressway)
  • About 60 minutes from central Sapporo
  • About 70 minutes from central Otaru
