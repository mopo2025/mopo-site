Otaru

Otaru Onze

Ocean views by day, city lights by night—your front-row seat near Sapporo.

Just 35 minutes from downtown Sapporo, Onze in Otaru’s Zenibako is known for easy access and being one of the area’s go-to night-ski spots. By day, wide-open slopes look out over Ishikari Bay; at sunset the water turns gold, and after dark the city glow and slope lighting set a dreamy, cinematic mood.

— Resort overview — by train & bus — by car

Resort overview

Onze has 10 runs for both skiers and snowboarders, with options from first-timers to advanced riders. On clear days you can take in Ishikari Bay and big skies—especially from the higher viewpoint runs, which feel like you’re gliding straight toward the sea. At night, full slope lighting flips the vibe, whether you’re on skis or a board.

While Onze is especially popular with snowboarders, it’s equally good for skiers to learn and lap. Beginners find a friendly setup; experienced riders can mix things up across varied lines. There’s a dedicated kids’ area with sledding and snow play, and on-site rentals and changing rooms make it easy to show up and go.

Heads-up: resort snowboard rentals come with fixed binding angles. For beginners that can make learning a touch harder—consider confirming in advance or renting off-site for a better fit.

The draw here is that singular feeling of riding toward the ocean and the glow of the city.

by train & bus

✦ JR Zenibako → free resort shuttle
From JR Sapporo Station, take a rapid train to Zenibako (about 21–27 minutes), then hop on the free shuttle to the resort (about 10 minutes). It’s an easy, straightforward connection.

by car

The resort has free parking for about 1,000 cars.

  • ~35 minutes from central Sapporo
  • ~20 minutes from central Otaru
  • ~70 minutes from New Chitose Airport
